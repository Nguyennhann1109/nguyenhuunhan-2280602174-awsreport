# AWS: Enterprise Cloud Architectures and Industry Application featuring Cloud Kinetics & Renova Cloud

## Event Information

- **Event Name:** AWS: Enterprise Cloud Architectures and Industry Application featuring Cloud Kinetics & Renova Cloud
- **Time:** 09:00, July 4, 2026
- **Location:** 26th Floor, Bitexco Financial Tower, 02 Hai Trieu Street, Ben Nghe Ward, District 1, Ho Chi Minh City
- **Format:** Online
- **Role:** Attendee

## Speaker Panel

- **Mr. Nguyen Gia Hung** – Head of Solutions Architect in Vietnam & Cambodia, Amazon Web Services
- **Mr. Khang Nguyen** – Solutions Architect, Cloud Kinetics
- **Ms. Nhu Tran** – Account Manager, Amazon Web Services 
- **Mr. Vinh Dinh** – Senior Data Engineer, Renova Cloud

## Event Objectives

This was not merely a theoretical workshop, but the third consecutive Study Tour organized by Swinburne Vietnam in collaboration with AWS and the FCAJ (First Cloud AI Journey) community. The core objective was to bridge the gap between academic theory and industry reality by connecting students directly with top-tier experts from AWS, Cloud Kinetics, and Renova Cloud. The event allowed us to step beyond basic Cloud Computing curriculum to grasp the panoramic view of how large enterprises operate their infrastructure in the real world.

## Key Content

### 1. The Cloud Market Landscape
A comprehensive look at the aggressive migration trends of massive, complex systems—such as Core Banking, insurance platforms, and booking engines—onto the cloud.

### 2. Enterprise Cloud Architectures
Deep-dive sessions on practical technology solutions presented by representatives from major AWS partners.

### 3. Career Development Strategy
Insights on personal positioning, understanding salary expectations relative to responsibilities, and the critical importance of building "visibility" within the industry.

### 4. Modern Professional Mindset
Techniques for refining inquiries, interdisciplinary teamwork, and cultivating persistent consistency.

## Major Takeaways

### 1. The "Brutal" Intern Standard
Mr. Hung (Head of SA at AWS) shared a real-world story about an intern applicant at a major bank who failed the interview simply for lacking proficiency in K8s (Kubernetes). This requirement, once reserved for Mid-level Engineers, underscores the fiercely competitive nature of today’s job market.

### 2. Unveiling the "Hidden Job Market"
Many of us wondered why quality Cloud roles rarely appear on mass job boards. The truth is that 90% to 100% of top-tier positions at Big Tech firms and major partners are filled via internal channels and referrals from within the community.

### 3. The "Pay it Forward" Spirit of FCAJ
An impressive transformation: 2/3 of the students who participated in the 2024 tour have returned in 2026 as core members and mentors, donning the "black shirts" to support and create opportunities for the next generation.

## Knowledge and Experience Gained

For someone accustomed to writing Spring Boot backend code or designing local SQL Server databases, this session provided a complete paradigm shift.

- **Professional Growth**: I realized that for real-world commercial products, a "feature-first" mindset is insufficient. To stay relevant, I must proactively master advanced infrastructure concepts, containerization, and Cloud Architecture early on.
- **Soft Skills**: The lesson on "refining" questions before seeking help from mentors was profound. Instead of asking generic questions, one should present specific assumptions to help the mentor guide them toward the correct solution. Furthermore, to undertake large projects, I must connect with peers in other fields like Business or Marketing to gain a multidimensional view before entering Hackathons.

## Reflections and Connections

- This trip provided a massive surge of energy, tempered by a healthy sense of pressure. Seeing students from Can Tho wake up at 5:00 AM to travel 180km to reach AWS made me realize the scale of competition from young people out there.
- The event shattered the outdated belief that "hiding away to study" is enough to succeed. To secure good opportunities, one must be proactive and engage with the community to increase self-visibility. As the speaker inspiringly concluded: Luck does not happen by chance; the more we act and immerse ourselves in the community, the more opportunities will find their way to us.
